About My Work

I'm currently pursuing my PhD at Texas A&M University, where I focus on optimization of energy systems and supply chains. My research bridges the gap between theoretical modeling and practical implementation in renewable energy integration.

With experience spanning from national-scale wind resource assessments in Egypt to off-grid hydrogen production systems in Spain, I work at the intersection of renewable energy technology, policy, and economic optimization.

My goal is to contribute to the global energy transition through rigorous research, innovative modeling approaches, and practical solutions that can be implemented at scale.

Research Areas

🌪️

Wind Energy Systems

I specialize in advanced wind resource assessment, turbine micrositing optimization, and large-scale wind farm integration. In my most recent role, I served as a senior wind resource analyst for 1,500 MW wind energy projects in Saudi Arabia.

📊

Hydrogen Supply Chains

PhD research focus on resilience modeling and optimization of green hydrogen production, storage, and distribution networks. Mathematical modeling of supply chain disruptions and recovery strategies.

⚡

Energy Storage Systems

Battery energy storage system optimization, grid services modeling, and hybrid renewable-storage system design. Research on diurnal and seasonal storage value in high-renewable scenarios.

☀️

Solar Energy Integration

Grid-scale solar PV system modeling, performance optimization, and economic analysis. Research on utility-scale solar integration with energy storage systems.

⚡

Energy System Modeling

Advanced mathematical modeling using PLEXOS, MATLAB, and optimization algorithms. Research on long-term energy planning under high renewable penetration scenarios.

📊

Energy Economics

Techno-economic analysis of renewable energy systems, LCOE optimization, and energy market modeling. Research on carbon pricing mechanisms and decarbonization strategies.
