Research Focus

My doctoral research at Texas A&M University centers on hydrogen supply chain resilience and optimization under uncertainty. I'm particularly interested in how renewable energy systems can be mathematically modeled and optimized to ensure reliable, cost-effective energy transition pathways. My work bridges theoretical optimization with practical implementation challenges, contributing to both academic understanding and real-world energy solutions.

Current Research

PhD Dissertation Research

Hydrogen Supply Chain Resilience Modeling

Developing mathematical optimization models to analyze and enhance the resilience of green hydrogen supply chains under various disruption scenarios. Focus on production, storage, and distribution network optimization.

In Progress

Energy System Optimization Under Uncertainty

Advanced stochastic optimization approaches for renewable energy system planning, incorporating uncertainty in resource availability, demand patterns, and policy frameworks.

In Progress

Wind Resource Analytics for Large-Scale Projects

Development of machine learning algorithms for wind resource assessment optimization, curtailment prediction, and early generation period analysis for utility-scale developments.

Hydrogen Systems Research

My primary research area focuses on mathematical modeling of hydrogen supply chains, with emphasis on resilience analysis, optimization under uncertainty, and integration with renewable energy systems. This work contributes to understanding how hydrogen can serve as a reliable energy carrier in a decarbonized energy system.

Optimization Methodologies
